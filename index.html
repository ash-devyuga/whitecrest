Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an immigration consultant to apply?

While it is not mandatory, working with a licensed immigration consultant significantly reduces the risk of errors, missed deadlines, and application refusals. We help ensure your case is presented accurately and strategically.

Is WhiteCrest a licensed consultancy?

Yes. Our consultants are licensed and regulated by the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants (CICC). You can verify our credentials through the CICC public register.

How long does the immigration process take?

Processing times vary by program. Express Entry draws can lead to PR in as little as six months, while family sponsorship may take twelve months or more. During your consultation, we provide realistic timelines based on your specific pathway.

What documents will I need to provide?

Required documents depend on the program but typically include identification, education credentials, language test results, proof of funds, and employment records. We provide a detailed checklist tailored to your application.

Can you help if my application was previously refused?

Absolutely. We regularly assist clients who have experienced prior refusals. We review the reasons for refusal, identify weaknesses, and build a stronger case for resubmission or explore alternative pathways.

How much does a consultation cost?

We offer an initial consultation at a competitive rate, which is credited toward your service package if you choose to proceed with us. Contact us for current pricing and availability.

Can I apply for permanent residency from inside Canada?

In many cases, yes. Programs like the Canadian Experience Class, certain PNP streams, and inland spousal sponsorship allow you to apply from within Canada. We can assess which options apply to your situation.

Do you offer virtual consultations?

Yes. We offer consultations via video call, phone, and in-person at our office. Many of our clients work with us entirely remotely, regardless of where they are in the world.
